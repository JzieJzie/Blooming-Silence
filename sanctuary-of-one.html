I slam the door—let thunder roll.
Let chaos claw, it gets no soul.
This room, this hush, this breath, this flame—
I name it mine. I stake my claim.

No clocks shall tick, no eyes shall pry,
No mask to wear, no need to lie.
The silence wraps me, dark and wide—
A blade, a balm, a shield, a guide.

The world can keep its hollow cheer,
Its noise, its hunger, painted fear.
Out there, I bend. I break. I yield.
But here—
I am the sword, the flame, the field.

These walls? Not walls—they are my skin.
And every shadow folds me in.
I walk alone, but not undone—
This is my throne.
Sanctuary of One.

I do not whisper here—I roar,
Without a sound, I split the floor.
The silence sings a deeper song—
Not weak. Not lost. But brutal. Strong.

Let them feast on hollow light,
On empty praise and sleepless nights—
I’ve carved from silence, stone by stone,
A kingdom vast,
And mine
alone.

Return?
